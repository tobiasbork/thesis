Principal component analysis (PCA) is an orthogonal transformation that seeks the directions of maximum variance in the data and is commonly used to reduce the dimensionality of the data. In image denoising, a compromise has to be found between noise reduction and preserving significant image details. PCA is a statistical technique for simplifying a dataset by reducing datasets to lower dimensions.
